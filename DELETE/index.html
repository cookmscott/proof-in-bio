Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about C2PA Viewer and content authenticity.

How do I verify content credentials?

To verify content credentials, upload your file to C2PA Viewer. The tool extracts and displays embedded C2PA data showing who created the content, what software was used, and whether it's been modified. Content credentials verification happens instantly in your browser with complete privacy - no server uploads required. Learn more about verifying content credentials.

What is C2PA Viewer?

C2PA Viewer is a free online tool for examining and verifying C2PA (Coalition for Content Provenance and Authenticity) data in digital files. It allows you to view the complete manifest output showing content authenticity information, as well as the raw manifest data in JSON format. The tool processes files entirely in your browser for privacy and security. Try it now.

How do I inspect C2PA metadata?

To inspect C2PA metadata, simply upload your file to our C2PA Viewer. The tool will extract and display the complete raw C2PA manifest in JSON format, including content credentials, action history, software used, AI generation markers, and cryptographic signatures. All inspection happens client-side in your browser with no server uploads required. Learn more about C2PA inspection.

What is C2PA Verify?

C2PA verify is the action of checking and validating C2PA manifest data to verify content credentials embedded in digital files. This verification process examines cryptographic signatures, confirms content credentials authenticity, and validates the provenance chain from creation to current state. It helps determine if content has been tampered with and reveals the content's provenance history.

What types of files can I verify?

C2PA Viewer supports JPEG, PNG, TIFF, WebP images, and MP4/MOV video files. All processing happens securely in your browser using WebAssembly technology.

Is my data secure?

Absolutely. We do not store your files. All processing happens in your browser, and your data never leaves your computer, ensuring complete privacy and security.

What does it mean if a file has no C2PA data?

The absence of C2PA data does not automatically mean a file is fake or has been tampered with. It simply means that its provenance has not been recorded using C2PA standards. This may be because the creation tool did not support C2PA or the feature was not enabled.
